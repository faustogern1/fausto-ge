About this tool

Digital Capabilities in the European Regions — data and methodology in brief

Digital industrial policy can have the side effect of exacerbating regional inequalities and leaving some areas behind. For this reason, it is important to design policies that are place-aware and aim to share the benefits of growth as widely as possible.

This explorer visualises the data produced for Mapping Digital Capabilities in the European Regions: A composite indicator of regional positioning across the digital stack, a project for the DG Regio of the European Commission. The project asks where in Europe the capacity to produce digital technologies is located, how that capacity differs across the layers of the digital stack, and where conditions for expansion are not yet matched by observed activity. It covers the 244 NUTS-2 regions of the EU-27.

The four layers of the digital stack

  • Raw materials — extraction and processing of the material inputs of digital technologies (mining, chemicals, basic metals and related processing).
  • Hardware — manufacture of electronic components, computers, machinery and related equipment.
  • Networks & connectivity — telecommunications infrastructure and services.
  • Software, data & AI — software publishing, programming, data services and related activities.

Three lenses

  • Current capability — observed digital activity: sectoral employment, patenting and high-growth firms.
  • Potential capability — enabling conditions for future activity: relatedness of the existing industrial mix, talent pipelines, energy costs, material deposits and access to finance.
  • Untapped potential — the conversion gap, defined as max(0, potential − current). High values flag regions whose conditions outpace their observed activity.

The regional typology

Regions are classified in two steps. A region is an Established leader if its current capability sits in the top decile of at least two layers. The remaining regions are split by the medians of average current capability and average untapped potential: Strong performers (current at or above the median), Latent hubs (current below the median, untapped at or above it) and Digital laggards (below both medians).

How the indices are built

All indicators are log-transformed and min–max normalised to [0,1] across the EU-27, so scores express relative position within the EU rather than absolute magnitudes. For each region and layer:

  • Current capability is the average of the employment, patenting and high-growth-firm indicators.
  • Potential capability averages relatedness with layer-specific enablers — talent (hardware, networks, software), electricity prices (reversed; raw materials and hardware) and critical-raw-material deposits (raw materials) — with access to finance entering every layer at half weight.
  • Untapped potential = max(0, potential − current).

Averages are computed on available components: missing indicators are dropped rather than treated as zeros. Cross-layer averages are the mean over the four layers.

Data sources

  • Employment — Eurostat Structural Business Statistics, regional employment in digital NACE divisions (reference year 2022).
  • Patenting — OECD REGPAT, patents allocated to regions by inventor address, priority years 2020–2024.
  • High-growth firms — Orbis (Bureau van Dijk) employment panel, Eurostat–OECD definition: at least 10 employees at the start of the window and at least 10% average annualised employment growth over three years, latest accounts 2022 or later. Counts are adjusted for cross-country differences in Orbis coverage against Eurostat business demography.
  • Talent — ETER, graduates in engineering and ICT fields (2023 extraction).
  • Electricity prices — Eurostat industrial electricity prices, 2024–2025 half-years, reversed so that lower prices score higher. Country level.
  • Material deposits — JRC data on critical raw material deposits.
  • Access to finance — EC/ECB SAFE survey (wave 36, 2025), share of SMEs identifying access to finance as their most important problem, reversed. Country level, half weight.
